Are you looking to support someone?

It can be hard seeing someone you care about experience mental health challenges or struggle with daily life. And it's natural to be unsure about what you can do to give them support. But there are practical things you can do that will make a difference.


You don't need any special training to show someone you care about them; sometimes, just being there can make all the difference.



Journey to Joy is an website where, connects you and those you care abourt to trusted help and support

Start a conversation

If you've noticed signs that someone you care for is having a hard time, it’s important to check in and ask them how they're going. We have useful tips on how to start a conversation with someone and how to support them.

Help them take action

Once you've had the conversation, and the person is ready, there are different things you can do to help them get the support they need – from getting informed to helping them make appointments to providing support during treatment.

Look after yourself

While caring for someone is rewarding, it can be physically and emotionally challenging. Don't forget to take some time to look after your own mental health and wellbeing.

Are you a parent or guardian of a young person?

If you are a parent or guardian caring for a young person with mental health challenges, it can be challenging and have an impact on you. Make time for self-care, lean on your support networks, and don't hesitate to reach out to professionals who can provide guidance and relief when you need it. You deserve to be supported, and it’s okay to ask for help for yourself as well.
It's important to make sure you're looking after yourself, too.
